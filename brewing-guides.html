Brewing Guides

Three reliable ways to make great coffee at home, from the simplest to the most technical.

Pour-Over

What You'll Need:

  • Pour-over dripper (V60 or Chemex)
  • Paper filter
  • Freshly ground coffee, medium-fine
  • Gooseneck kettle
  • Digital scale

Steps:

  1. Rinse the paper filter with hot water to remove papery taste.
  2. Add 20g of ground coffee to the filter.
  3. Pour just enough water to saturate the grounds and wait 30 seconds for the bloom.
  4. Slowly pour the remaining water in a circular motion over 2–3 minutes.
  5. Let it fully drain, then serve immediately.

French Press

What You'll Need:

  • French press
  • Coarsely ground coffee
  • Kettle
  • Timer

Steps:

  1. Add coarse coffee grounds to the empty press.
  2. Pour hot water evenly over the grounds and stir gently.
  3. Place the lid on top without pressing down, and steep for 4 minutes.
  4. Press the plunger down slowly and evenly.
  5. Pour immediately to avoid over-extraction.

Espresso

What You'll Need:

  • Espresso machine
  • Fine, evenly ground coffee
  • Tamper
  • Digital scale

Steps:

  1. Dose 18g of finely ground coffee into the portafilter.
  2. Tamp evenly with firm, level pressure.
  3. Lock the portafilter in and start the shot immediately.
  4. Aim for a 25–30 second extraction time.
  5. Stop the shot once you reach roughly double the dry weight in liquid.

Method Comparison

Method Brew Time Best For Difficulty
Pour-Over 3–4 minutes Clean, bright single-origin flavors Medium
French Press 4–5 minutes Full-bodied, rich cups Easy
Espresso 25–30 seconds Concentrated shots, milk-based drinks Hard
